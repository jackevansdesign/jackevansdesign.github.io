Open source the ocean

Undercurrent is an open source digital identity for ocean exploration at National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration.

As a federal government contractor, I wrote, designed, and coded these brief guidelines on typography, colors, images, and graphics.

“Open source the ocean” — a proposed mission statement for ocean exploration — guided my attempt to shift oceanexplorer.noaa.gov toward a photojournalistic direction. Instead of producing longform pieces in the “captain’s log” format, I created a new digital identity powered by high-fidelity, open source imagery.

For example, our photos would often “go viral” on sites like Buzzfeed and The Huffington Post with significantly less public interest via our own online channels. By similarly repackaging two or three highlight images with rigorously edited microcopy, I asserted we might recreate and sustain this level of engagement ourselves.

While the guidelines were, as a whole, too radical a shift for key NOAA stakeholders, elements of the project informed my work on explicitly public facing microsites for World Oceans Day and the Annual Report.
